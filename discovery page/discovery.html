Resources on Campus

Looking for ways to improve your self-efficacy? Check-out these resources! Most of the resources below are cost-free for University of Washington Students!

Counseling Center

UW Counseling center is a great place to start if you have any questions or concerns about your own self-efficacy. They offer mental health resources, Skills-based workshops, individual and group counseling as well as crisis services

CSE Advising Counseling

Paul G Allen school of Computer Science and engineering also have their resources on their advising and counseling page, under the personal counseling section

Let's Talk

Let's Talk is a program that connects UW students with support from experienced counselors from the Counseling Center and HallHealth Center without an appointment. Counselors hold drop-in hours at various sites on campus

Live Well

LiveWell empowers students with the wellness skills and knowledge to succeed during their time at UW and beyond. They support the personal and social development of students by providing evidence-based advocacy, consultation, deucation and training. See more information here

Academic Support Programs

Our programs help UW students realize their potentials- at the University and beyond. We provide a pace for all students to be included, challenged and supported in their educational journey.

Hall Health Center

Hall Health provides high-quality, evidence- based mental health care to UW's diverse student body. We offer a range of services to assess and tereat mental health concerns We encourage you to seek care with us even if you're not quite sure what you're looking for or if you "need" help

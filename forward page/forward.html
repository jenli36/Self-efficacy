Have you ever have a moment when you doubted your educational skill sets? You feel like you don't belong to where you at and you are not smart enough or simply not good enough?

The experience you had is closely tied to the idea of “self-efficacy”. This is what we are trying to address through our project! Self-efficacy is the belief we have in our own abilities, specifically our ability to meet the challenges ahead of us and complete a task successfully!

Studies show that compared with students who doubt their learning capabilities, those who feel efficacious for learning or performing a task participate development of academic self-efficacy more readily, work harder, persist longer when they encounter difficulties and achieve at a higher level.

Within competitive majors, you are in (CSE) low self-efficacy is prevalent among students. The stair installations, which can be found in Gates Centers, show the volume just how many people struggle with low-self-efficacy in hopes to bring awareness to encourage dialogue about low self-efficacy.

We have gathered some information about the definition of self-efficacy, negative-effects of low self-efficacy, and resources regarding self-efficacy. We hope that the information will be helpful for you all as you advance forward in your career and academic pursue.

Please share or refer this to your friends to help each other overcome low-self-efficacy.

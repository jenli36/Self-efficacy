Have you ever doubted your educational skill sets? Or felt like you don't belong and are not smart enough in your academic cohort?

The experience you had is closely tied to the idea of “low self-efficacy”, a topic we are trying to raise awareness of and address through our project. Self-efficacy is the belief we have in our own abilities, specifically our ability to meet the challenges ahead of us and complete a task successfully!

Within competitive majors, such as the CSE department, you are in (CSE) low self-efficacy is prevalent among students. The stair installations, which can be found in the Gates Centers, show the volume just how many people struggle with low-self-efficacy in hopes to bring awareness to encourage dialogue about low self-efficacy.

Why should you care about self-efficacy?

Studies show that compared to students who doubt their learning capabilities, those with a higher sense of self-efficacy have the ability to work harder and persist longer when they encounter difficulties and achieve at a higher level.

As you explore our website, you will find more information about self-efficacy, the negative effects and symptoms of low self-efficacy, and resources to help overcome improve your self-efficacy.We hope that you will find this information useful as you advance forward in your career and academic pursuits.

Please share this website to your friends to help each other overcome and encourage dialogue about low-self-efficacy.
